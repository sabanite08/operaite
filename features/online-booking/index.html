How it works

Three pieces. Five-minute setup.

1
Set up your booking page
Pick a URL slug (operaite.net/book/your-business), list the services you offer, set your contact email and phone. Done in 5 minutes.
2
Paste the URL into Google Business Profile
GBP has a dedicated “Book Online” field. Customers searching “HVAC near me” or “plumber [city]” see a Book button right in the Maps result. Also drop the URL in your email signature, social bio, and business card QR.
3
Approve or decline each request
Customer fills name, contact, service, preferred time, notes. You see it in your inbox, click Approve or Decline. Customer gets an automatic confirmation email; you get the appointment on your calendar.
Why approval-based, not Calendly-style

Trades don’t want strangers self-booking.

Calendly works for SaaS sales calls and haircuts. For service trades, you need to vet the customer’s location, scope, and equipment list before committing.

🎯
You stay in control
Customer requests, you approve. No 4 a.m. bookings from people outside your service area. No surprise jobs that don’t fit your schedule.
📍
Service-area filtering
If the request is 90 minutes outside your zone, decline it (or counter-propose a service-call premium). The customer gets a clean apology email with your direct contact.
📋
Notes capture scope upfront
The booking form asks for notes (“AC unit is 2018 Carrier 3-ton, makes a clicking sound on startup, second floor”) so you can quote accurately before driving out.
🔄
Same scheduler, both inputs
Your existing manually-entered jobs and new approved bookings live in the same calendar. No two systems to keep in sync.
📧
Customer emails are automatic
“Request received” on submit. “Confirmed” on approve. “Couldn’t fit your time” on decline. All branded, all reply-to-you.
💬
Customer replies route to you
When the customer hits Reply on a confirmation, the email goes straight to your business email — not to Operaite. So you actually have a thread to work from.

Questions

Frequently asked.

Is this like Calendly?
Different problem. Calendly is instant self-serve booking — built for SaaS sales calls and haircuts. Operaite Online Booking is approval-based — customers REQUEST a time, you confirm or suggest alternatives. That fits how trades actually work: you need to vet location, scope, and equipment before committing.
Where do customers find my booking link?
The biggest channel is Google Business Profile’s “Book Online” button — drives 60-70% of trade leads. Also: email signature, business card QR, social bio, missed-call auto-text, and embedded button on your website (widget on roadmap).
How fast does the customer get a confirmation?
Two emails. “We received your request” immediately on submit. “Your appointment is confirmed” when you approve. Both reply-to-you so the customer can message back directly.
Can multiple customers book the same slot?
Today, yes — the booking system is request-based, not real-time-availability-based. If two customers request the same slot, you approve one and counter-offer the other. Real-time availability is on the roadmap.
Does approving sync to my calendar?
Yes — approved requests sync into your Operaite scheduler so you see the appointment alongside your manually-entered jobs. Customer info also flows into your invoicing module.
Is this a free tool or paid?
Online Booking is part of the paid Operaite app ($29/mo, 7-day free trial). Unlimited bookings, no per-booking fee.
